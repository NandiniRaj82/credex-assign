## FAQ

**Q: How does it work?**
A: You enter which AI tools you pay for, which plan, how many seats, and your actual monthly spend. Our audit engine checks whether you're on the right plan for your team size and use case, flags excess seats, identifies cheaper alternatives with equivalent capability, and calculates your total savings opportunity. It takes about 2 minutes.

**Q: Is this actually free? What's the catch?**
A: Completely free. StackLens is built by Credex — a company that sells discounted AI infrastructure credits. When the audit finds significant savings, we'll mention Credex as one way to save even more. For smaller stacks, we're just a free tool with no pitch. The product earns trust first.

**Q: Where does my data go?**
A: Your tool and spending data is stored in your browser (localStorage) while you fill out the form. When you submit the audit, the tool and savings data is stored to generate your shareable URL — your email and company name are never included in that public record. If you enter your email to receive the report, it's stored securely and used only to send you the audit summary.

**Q: How accurate is the pricing data?**
A: We verify every price against the official vendor pricing page before each update. Dates are listed in PRICING_DATA.md. AI tool pricing changes often — we check weekly. If you spot an error, email us.

**Q: What if my company uses a negotiated enterprise price?**
A: For Enterprise/custom-priced plans, the audit engine can't reverse-engineer your contract. Instead, it tells you the next-lower plan's list price so you can benchmark whether your enterprise rate is competitive. Most enterprise customers find they're paying 10–30% above the Business tier equivalent — which is the entry point for a Credex conversation.
